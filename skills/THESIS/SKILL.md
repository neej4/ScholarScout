# Thesis-Oriented Research

## Profile
- **Duration:** 4-12 months (undergraduate) or 12-24 months (master's)
- **Compute:** Depends on topic
- **Budget:** Usually limited (student budget)
- **Scope:** Must fill 40-100 pages with structured chapters

## Constraints
- Must be supervised (advisor approval needed)
- Must demonstrate understanding of methodology (not just results)
- Must include comprehensive literature review (20+ references minimum)
- Must show original contribution (even if incremental)
- Must be defensible in oral examination

## Structure Requirements
- Chapter 1: Introduction (problem, motivation, objectives, scope)
- Chapter 2: Literature Review (theoretical framework, prior work)
- Chapter 3: Methodology (detailed, reproducible)
- Chapter 4: Results and Discussion
- Chapter 5: Conclusion and Future Work
- References + Appendices

## Quality Bar
- Literature review: comprehensive coverage of the sub-field
- Methodology: clearly justified choices, not just "I used X because it's popular"
- Results: honest reporting including failures and limitations
- Discussion: interpretation, comparison with prior work, implications
- Future work: concrete next steps, not vague "more research needed"

## Output Expectations
- Thesis document (40-100 pages)
- Defense presentation (15-30 minutes)
- Code/data repository (if applicable)
- 1-page abstract for university records
- Optional: conference paper extracted from thesis

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid
- Scope too broad (trying to solve everything)
- No clear research question
- Literature review that's just a list (no synthesis)
- Results without interpretation
- Conclusion that just repeats the abstract
