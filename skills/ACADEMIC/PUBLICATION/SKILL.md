# Publication-Oriented Research

## Profile
- **Duration:** 3-8 months (conference deadline driven)
- **Compute:** Whatever the method requires
- **Budget:** Flexible
- **Scope:** Novel contribution → experiments → paper → submission

## Constraints
- Must have clear novelty claim (what's new vs prior work)
- Must include comparison with state-of-the-art baselines
- Must target a specific venue (conference or journal)
- Scope must fit within page limits (8-page conference or 12-page journal)
- Reproducibility: code and data should be releasable

## Quality Bar
- Novelty: at least one of (new method, new application, new insight, new dataset)
- Experiments: minimum 3 baselines, 2 datasets, ablation study
- Writing: clear problem statement, related work positioning, limitation discussion
- Significance: "so what?" must be answerable in one sentence

## Target Venues (by field)
- CS/AI: NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, AAAI, ACL, CVPR, EMNLP
- Medicine: Lancet, NEJM, BMJ, JAMA (or specialty journals)
- Engineering: IEEE Transactions, Nature Engineering
- Interdisciplinary: Nature, Science, PNAS, Nature Communications

## Output Expectations
- Camera-ready paper (LaTeX, conference template)
- Supplementary materials (appendix, code, data)
- Rebuttal preparation (anticipate reviewer questions)
- Presentation slides or poster

## Timeline Template
- Week 1-2: Literature survey, identify gap
- Week 3-4: Method design, initial experiments
- Week 5-8: Full experiments, ablations
- Week 9-10: Writing, figures, tables
- Week 11-12: Internal review, revision, submission
