# PhD Research Projects

## Profile
- **Duration:** 1-3 years (single contribution thread)
- **Compute:** Institutional cluster, multi-GPU, large-scale
- **Budget:** Research grants, institutional resources
- **Scope:** Novel contribution to the field — new method, theory, or paradigm

## What Makes a Good PhD Project
- Addresses a fundamental limitation in current approaches
- Requires theoretical insight (not just engineering)
- Produces multiple publications (1 top venue + 2-3 workshops)
- Advances the state of the art on established benchmarks
- Has broader impact beyond the specific task

## Typical Structure
1. Comprehensive literature survey (3-6 months)
2. Identify fundamental limitation (2-3 months)
3. Propose theoretical framework (3-6 months)
4. Implement and validate (6-12 months)
5. Large-scale experiments (3-6 months)
6. Write dissertation chapters (ongoing)

## Resource Constraints
- Model size: unlimited (70B+, custom architectures)
- Dataset: custom collection, annotation campaigns
- Training: weeks on multi-GPU clusters
- May require specialized hardware (TPUs, neuromorphic chips)
- Collaboration with other labs/researchers expected
